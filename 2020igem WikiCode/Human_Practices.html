PDCA cycle

PDCA cycle was first proposed by Walter A. Shewhart, an American quality management expert. He divided quality management into plan, do, check and Act. PDCA requires us to make a plan, implement the plan, check the effectiveness of the implementation, and then include the successful ones into the criteria, and leave the unsuccessful ones to the next cycle to solve. Our project has used this mature framework from the start to ensure the smooth progress of our project without missing a single step. Marketing Research

The environmental pollution caused by adhesive bandage and the high-end wound dressings that combine adhesive bandage and antibacterial functions mentioned by firemen have attracted our attention. After we did some investigations about the environmental pollution caused by medical wastes in our lives and as we mentioned in our interview that the market conditions for this type of dressing used in our project, we made the following summary.

Medical waste

Refers to the direct or indirect infectious, toxic and other hazardous waste produced by medical and health institutions in medical treatment, prevention, health care and other related activities.It contains a large number of infectious pathogens, the hazard is significantly higher than ordinary household garbage,If management is lax or improperly handled, medical waste can easily cause pollution to water, soil and air.Can easily become the source of the spread of the virus, cause the spread of the epidemic.

Items contaminated by patients’ blood, body fluids and excreta, including: cotton balls, cotton swabs, drainage slivers, gauze and various other dressings are all infectious waste in medical waste.Discarded general medicines, such as antibiotics, are all medical wastes in medical waste.

Current status of medical waste treatment

With the increase of population and medical institutions and the improvement of medical conditions, the amount of medical waste generated is increasing year by year.The damage is more serious and the incidence rate increases.And the medical waste generated has increased, such a vicious circle, the social losses caused will be immeasurable.

Among the many medical waste treatment methods currently used internationally, the high-temperature incineration treatment method is the first,The reason is that this technology has many advantages, such as wide application range, thorough disinfection and sterilization, significant volume reduction effect, complete relevant standards and specifications, and mature technology.It has been widely used internationally for many years.

Defects of incineration technology

The main advantage of incineration technology is that the volume and weight are significantly reduced, and the waste is obviously destroyed.But its biggest disadvantage is serious air pollution.Prone to produce highly toxic substances such as dioxins, polycyclic aromatic compounds, polychlorinated biphenyls, and harmful gases such as hydrogen chloride, hydrogen fluoride and sulfur dioxide, a complete exhaust gas purification system needs to be equipped, and the incineration fly ash contains a lot of heavy metals; In addition, the cost of incineration is high, and the incinerator needs frequent maintenance and has a short service life.

Development status of global high-end wound dressing products

In recent years, the global medical dressing market has basically remained stable. With the increasingly problem of global population aging and the resulting increase in patients with ulcers and other diseases, the global medical dressing market demand will continue to grow.

From the perspective of development trends, consumers’ demand for multi-functional, new-material medical dressings is becoming increasingly urgent。

High-end wound dressings are mainly targeted at wounds that are difficult to heal, take a long time, are more painful for patients, and may cause serious complications.High-end wound dressings mainly has the advantages of reducing the pain of drug change, shortening the healing time, reducing the number of drug changes and so on.

According to BMI Research, the global high-end dressing market reached $5,595 million in 2017 and is expected to reach $7,015 million in 2022.The new high-end dressing market has broad prospects and is the current trend of industry development.

What should we do?

The polyhydroxy fatty acid ester (PHA) we use is a new type of degradable material with a very special degradation method.After use, PHA can be completely degraded into β-hydroxybutyric acid, carbon dioxide and water in the body.It has its advantages in performance, practicability, degradability and safety.

In terms of performance,PHA can meet or exceed the performance of traditional plastics in certain specific areas;In terms of practicality,It has application performance and sanitary performance similar to similar traditional materials;In terms of degradability, after PHA is used, it can be degraded quickly in the natural environment, and become a substance and non-toxic gas that are easily used by the environment, reduce the impact on the environment;In terms of safety, the substances produced or remaining in the degradation process aren’t harmful to the environment, will not affect the survival of humans and other creatures.

Academic communication with Professor Han

In the initial stage, we considered to use six kinds of antimicrobial peptides with their own characteristics. Among them, HBD3,HD5,HD5d5,DCD1L is human antimicrobial peptides. ACD (allergic contact dermatitis) is a common but complex skin disease. Its common symptoms are local erythema, swelling, papules, etc. And if it’s serious, it can spread from the contact around the emergence of toxic epidermal necrosis or granulomatous lesions, accompanied by systemic symptoms, such as headache, fever and so on. In order to reduce the possibility of ACD caused by allergen contact with wound, our team selected human-friendly antimicrobial peptides by searching many literatures and asking teachers in the process of selecting antimicrobial peptides. In addition, we also selected two artificially designed antimicrobial peptides, P5 and P6.2, which were analyzed by biological software to delete some meaningless sequences on the basis of the original antimicrobial peptides and improve their active domains to enhance their functions. And we also used the methods of synthetic biology to express and verify its antibacterial activity afterward.

In the middle of our experiment, we encountered some difficulties about our experiment. The electrophoretic bands of antimicrobial peptides were very clear but could not be purified. For this reason, we specially interviewed Han Jing, a professor in the Chinese Academy of Sciences and she has rich experience for this. She analyzed that antimicrobial peptides may exist in the form of inclusion bodies and in bacteria in the form of precipitation. It existed very little in the supernatant. But the process is very difficult for us, if we need to purify inclusion body protein. So we intended to get as much protein as possible to be expressed in the supernatant by adjusting the concentration and time.

In the later, professor Han suggested that we can make PHA electrospinning to enhance its elasticity and air permeability. So it will be more suitable for us as an adhesive bandage material and can be affixed to the joint for facilitate movement.
